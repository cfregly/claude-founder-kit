# External Proof Plan: Claude Idea to PMF

Status: candidate protocol.

This plan exists to test both the startup idea and the founder-kit. Nothing here counts as external proof until skeptical users run it on their own workflows and leave receipts.

## Claim Under Test

Claude Idea to PMF helps a first-time founder move from raw idea to better external buyer evidence than their baseline process.

The founder-kit claim under test is narrower:

The kit can force a useful wedge, experiment, launch motion, and value receipt without hiding uncertainty.

## Minimum External Proof

Run three skeptical-user sessions.

| Session | User | Baseline | Founder-kit stage | Value axis | Pass condition |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | first-time nontechnical founder | their current idea and planned next step | Idea | clarity | user changes buyer, workflow, metric, or kill criteria because the output caught a weakness |
| 2 | startup advisor or operator | manual critique of the idea | Idea plus Launch | decision quality | reviewer agrees at least one output is worth using and records one objection |
| 3 | builder or agent reviewer | proposed Paperclip-backed architecture | MVP harden | risk caught | reviewer accepts at least one risk or boundary fix before build |

## Before And After Packet

Each session gets a folder with:

- `baseline.md`: the user's raw idea, current next step, and what they think will prove demand
- `objections.md`: what would make the kit output useless, misleading, or not worth using
- `commands.md`: exact commands, commit SHA, and input files
- `output.md`: raw kit output or linked artifacts
- `decision.md`: use, change, or reject, with the reason
- `follow_up.md`: what changed after the session

## Rejection Is Allowed

The external user can reject the output for any concrete reason:

- buyer is still too broad
- outreach would embarrass them or misrepresent the offer
- output hides the hardest assumption
- action is not feasible in their current week
- risk finding is obvious or low value
- the system made them feel productive without creating buyer evidence

Rejected receipts stay in the repo. They are evidence.

## Pilot Script

1. Ask the user to write their raw idea and planned next step without coaching.
2. Ask them what would make an AI founder tool useless for this task.
3. Run the relevant founder-kit stage.
4. Ask them to mark every useful, wrong, generic, and risky part.
5. Require a decision: use, change, or reject.
6. If they use or change anything, record the exact before/after.
7. If they reject it, record what would need to change.

## First Product Wedge To Test

Do not test "AI company operating system." Test:

> In seven days, help first-time founders send five buyer-specific outreach notes and make one continue, narrow, or kill decision from real replies.

Paperclip can power the workflow underneath, but the customer-facing promise is buyer evidence.
