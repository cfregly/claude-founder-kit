# Claude Idea to PMF

Claude Idea to PMF helps first-time, nontechnical founders turn a raw startup idea into buyer evidence in seven days. The first wedge is not "build your company with agents." It is "get five real buyer conversations or one kill-quality rejection before you build."

The first buyer is a domain expert with a real problem and no product team. Examples include a nurse with a staffing workflow idea, a contractor with a quoting problem, a teacher with a parent communication workflow, or an accountant with a client intake process. They can name the people with the pain, but they do not know how to test demand or scope a product.

The activation event is simple: the founder sends five buyer-specific outreach notes, logs the responses, and chooses one of three next moves: continue, narrow, or kill. The product does not let them skip the buyer evidence step.

The workflow:

1. Capture the founder's idea, background, constraints, and reachable buyer network.
2. Force a narrow buyer and painful workflow.
3. Generate a customer discovery script that avoids pitching.
4. Generate five buyer-specific outreach notes.
5. Track replies, objections, and calls.
6. Turn real evidence into a prototype brief, concierge workflow, or kill decision.

The product is opinionated. It rewards evidence, not enthusiasm. It marks synthetic personas, generic market research, and pretty landing pages as weak evidence unless real buyers respond.

Paperclip can run the agent company underneath: idea coach, interview coach, prototype builder, growth operator, and skeptical reviewer. Paperclip is useful because goals, tasks, approvals, budgets, and audit logs matter. The defensibility is not the model. It is the workflow of record for evidence, the founder's buyer graph, and the history of objections and commitments that compounds across experiments.

Why not just use Claude, ChatGPT, or a startup template? Because the buyer evidence loop is the product. A general chatbot will help you polish the idea. This system keeps score on whether real people with the problem responded, objected, paid, or changed the next experiment.

The first seven-day metric is not revenue. It is validated external signal:

- five relevant buyer outreaches sent
- three replies or two calls booked
- one clear continue, narrow, or kill decision
- every decision traces to buyer evidence, not AI confidence

The first paid offer is a guided PMF sprint for first-time founders: $99 for a seven-day idea-to-evidence run, with optional concierge help to write outreach and prepare the first demo.

The platform risk story is that the product owns the workflow of record for founder evidence: outreach, replies, calls, objections, experiments, and decisions. That history is private, contextual, and useful only when tied to the founder's actual buyer network. The model provider can generate advice, but it does not own the founder's evidence trail or enforce the anti-false-confidence workflow.
