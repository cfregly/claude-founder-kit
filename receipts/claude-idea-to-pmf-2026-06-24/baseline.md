# Baseline: Claude Idea to PMF

Status: candidate startup idea.

Date: 2026-06-24.

Founder prompt:

> Build a system that helps common folks take their idea to product-market fit. Build something like Polsia using open-source tools like Paperclip, perhaps.

## Starting Hypothesis

Everyday founders do not mainly need another chatbot. They need a guided path from a raw idea to external buyer evidence. The product should help them narrow the buyer, run customer discovery, create a small proof artifact, and decide whether to continue, change, or kill the idea.

## Raw Product Shape

The system acts like a founder operating room:

- capture the idea, founder context, constraints, and why the founder is credible
- turn the idea into a narrow buyer, problem, and risky assumption
- generate customer discovery scripts and outreach
- track buyer conversations and objections
- help build a small prototype, demo, or concierge workflow
- decide the next experiment from real evidence

Paperclip may be the control plane underneath because it has goals, agents, budgets, approvals, and audit trails. The customer should not need to know that. They should feel a clear path from idea to evidence.

## Skeptical Starting Objections

- "Common folks" is too broad to sell to.
- "Take an idea to PMF" is too big to promise.
- The product may attract people who want validation, not truth.
- AI-generated personas, decks, and landing pages can create false confidence.
- Paperclip may make the build heavier before the workflow is proven.
- PMF cannot be automated. The system can only force better experiments and evidence capture.

## Baseline Decision Process

Without the kit, the likely next move would be to build an agentic founder dashboard and demo it. That risks building the operating system before proving that a specific founder segment will use it to get real customer signal.

## Baseline Bar

The first external proof should not be "the product seems useful." It should be:

- a real user started with a raw idea
- they recorded their existing next step before using the system
- they used the system to produce a narrower experiment
- they were free to reject the output
- they either used the output with real buyers or changed the idea because the output caught a flaw
